Chapati, also known as roti, rooti, rotli, rotta, safati, shabaati, phulka, chapo, poli, and roshi, is an unleavened flatbread originating from the Indian subcontinent and staple in India, Nepal, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, East Africa, Arabian Peninsula and the Caribbean.

NORTH SIDE

Couscous – sometimes called kusksi or kseksu – is a traditional North African dish of small steamed granules of rolled semolina that is often served with a stew spooned on top.

CENTRAL SIDE

Central African cuisine includes the cuisines, cooking traditions, practices, ingredients and foods of the Central African Republic. Indigenous agriculture in the country includes millet, sorgum, banana, yam, okra, yellow onion, garlic, spinach, rice and palm oil.

Bobotie is a well-known South African dish consisting of spiced minced meat baked with an egg-based topping.
